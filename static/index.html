WCAG Compliance Rules

Alt Text Rules (20 WCAG Guidelines)

  1. No 'image of': Avoid words like 'image of', 'picture of', 'photo of'
  2. Avoid commas: Use commas only when grammatically necessary
  3. Keep short: Short alt text must be 5–12 words
  4. No full stops: Simple alt text should not end with a period
  5. No extra adjectives: Avoid descriptive words like 'beautiful', 'lovely', 'amazing'
  6. No repetition: Don't repeat the same information
  7. No keyword stuffing: Avoid spam keywords
  8. Use lowercase: Keep alt text in lowercase
  9. Avoid articles: Skip 'the', 'a', 'an' unless needed for grammar
  10. No assumptions: Describe only what is visible
  11. Main subject: Focus on the primary content
  12. Mention action: Include action verbs for functional images
  13. Include text: Always include visible text and labels
  14. No special characters: Avoid symbols except periods, commas, apostrophes
  15. Avoid abbreviations: Spell out full words
  16. Natural word order: Maintain normal sentence structure
  17. No emojis: Exclude all emoji characters
  18. No color references: Never mention colors or color-related words
  19. Context aware: Consider provided captions and context
  20. Clear & concise: Be brief and to the point

Formatting Rules

Short Alt Text

  • Start with "A" or "An" article
  • Keep to 5–12 words exactly
  • No period at the end
  • Use simple present tense
  • Example: a person typing on a laptop

Long Alt Text

  • Start with "The" article
  • Include complete details
  • End with a period
  • Use simple present tense
  • Don't repeat short alt text details
  • Example: The workspace shows a person seated at a desk typing on a modern laptop with multiple monitors displaying code.

What NOT to Include

  • ❌ Color names or descriptions: red, blue, dark, light, vibrant, muted
  • ❌ Introductory phrases: "The image shows", "This shows", "Image of"
  • ❌ Abbreviations: use "and" instead of "&", spell out "approximately"
  • ❌ Special characters: hyphens, brackets, colons, mathematical symbols
  • ❌ Assumptions or inferences not visible in the image
  • ❌ Repetitive information
